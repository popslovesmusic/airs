## Section 4: IGSOA-SIM Interest & Evaluation

**Brief Product Description (shown to respondents):**

> IGSOA-SIM is a high-performance simulation platform for nonlinear dynamics, integrating generalized stochastic operators with advanced physics engines. It offers:
> - **GNU-licensed tools:** CLI, Python/Julia analysis scripts, mission generator, documentation (FREE)
> - **Commercial engines:** 7 optimized C++ engines (iGSOA-Complex, iGSOA-Real, Quantum, Plasma, 2D/3D variants)
> - **Academic license:** Free for verified .edu users
> - **Performance:** Fast C++ core with FFTW, multi-language integration
> - **Flexibility:** Template-based missions, automated parameter sweeps, batch processing

## Analysis Plan

### Key Metrics to Track:

1. **Target Market Validation:**
   - % interested (Q12 score 4-5)
   - % likely to try (Q20 score 4-5)
   - Net Promoter Score (Q23)

2. **Pricing Validation:**
   - Median acceptable price (Q15)
   - Budget availability (Q7)
   - Willingness to pay by segment

3. **Feature Priorities:**
   - Critical features (Q10)
   - Top rankings (Q17)
   - Additional engines needed (Q18)

4. **Competitive Position:**
   - Current tool satisfaction (Q6)
   - Pain points (Q9)
   - Switch motivators (Q16)
   - Barriers to adoption (Q24, Q26)

5. **Beta Program Uptake:**
   - % willing to beta test (Q21)
   - Email collection rate
   - Geographic distribution

### Success Criteria:

**GO Decision (proceed with commercial launch):**
- ≥30% respondents "very interested" (Q12 = 5)
- ≥40% would try immediately/likely (Q20 ≥ 4)
- Median acceptable price ≥ $500/year
- ≥50 qualified beta testers

**NO-GO Decision (pivot or delay):**
- <15% interested
- <20% would try
- Median price < $250
- <20 beta testers

**PIVOT Indicators:**
- High interest but pricing mismatch
- Feature gaps identified
- Wrong target market
- Adjust and re-survey
